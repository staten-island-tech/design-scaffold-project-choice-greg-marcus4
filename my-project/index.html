Transportation


France has some of the oldest transportation systems. It one of the densest networks in the world, with 146 kilometers of roads and 6.2 kilometers of rail lines per 100 kilometers squared. The center of it is in one of the most busy cities, the city of Paris. Railroads, roads, air, and water, all sorts of transportation are widely developed in France.

1. Railroads

Railroads in France have been used for a long time. It all started with the French engineer Pierre Michel Moisson-Desroches suggesting to the Emperor Napoleon to build seven national railways from Paris in order to travel short distances quickly. However, The first railway in France would be opened in 1827, with permission of King Louis XVIII in 1823. It went from the city of Saint-Étienne to the commune of Andrézieux-Bouthéon. The network has undergone a major modernization since 1981 with the arrival of the TGV high-speed rail service which has been consistently expanded in following years.
